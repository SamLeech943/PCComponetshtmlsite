Power Supply Unit


The Power supply unit or the PSU converts mains AC to regulated DC power for the internal components of a computer.
Most modern computers use switched-mode power supplies, this means that it has a more effective and efficient way of convertin power.
the power converted is passed around the PC giving power to the different components, similarly to how blood pumps around a human body.



Examples of Some Power supplies





Power supplies also come in two different varients, one being fixed wires and modular,
On a non modular power supply, all wires are attached and you just plug in the ones that you need for your system.
In a modular power system, no wires are attached to the PSU meaninig you only need to connect the relevent wires, this can improve cable managemnent
and increases airflow and power efficiency
