Graphics Card


The Graphics card or GPU is an essential part of any computer system it's primary job is to process image data.
Graphcs Cards come in all shapes and sizes, they also come with different memory sizes which in modern graphics cards can range from anywhere from 1GB to 8GB.
This memory is refered to as Vram or Virtual RAM, it serves as a temporary holding area for video data as the GPU generates each image,
Because the graphics card doesn't need to share memory with the main processor.

     

Examples of Some Graphics Cards






Graphics Cards are connected to the motherboard via the PCI-E connector.
Most modern Graphics cards also come with a 6 pin or 8 pin conector that also connects to the Power Supply and provides more power to a high end GPU.
GPU's are very different as many third party comapnies such as MSI and Asus make their own versions of popular Graphics cards such as the Nvidia GTX 1080.
